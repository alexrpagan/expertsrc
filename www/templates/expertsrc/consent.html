Consent to Participate in Non-Biomedical Research

Expert-User Analysis For Data Tamer

You are asked to participate in a research study conducted by Michael Stonebraker (Ph.D.) and Alexander Pagan (B.S.) from the department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (M.I.T.). You were selected as a possible participant in this study because you are have experience working with Pharon assay data. You should read the information below, and ask questions about anything you do not understand, before deciding whether or not to participate.

PARTICIPATION AND WITHDRAWAL

Your participation in this study is completely voluntary and you are free to choose whether to be in it or not. If you choose to be in this study, you may subsequently withdraw from it at any time without penalty or consequences of any kind. The investigator may withdraw you from this research if circumstances arise which warrant doing so.

PURPOSE OF THE STUDY

The purpose of this study is to gauge the effectiveness of a human-aided data integration system called Data Tamer, which was created at M.I.T. Particularly, we wish to assess the effectiveness of the system’s mechanisms for sending data curation tasks to a group of expert users.

The results of this study will be used to improve the quality of the NIBR Pharon data management systems.

PROCEDURES

The study will occur over the course of 3 weeks. During each week of the study, you will be asked for no more than 70 minutes of active involvement. If you volunteer to participate in this study, we would ask you to do the following things:

  • Learn about Data Tamer and data integration (10 minutes):
    • Read a short introduction to data integration.
    • Read a short introduction to Data Tamer’s functionality
  • Complete data integration tasks (varies: fewer than 60 minutes per week):
    • Use Data Tamer’s user interface to answer a series of simple questions.
  • Complete a questionnaire (5 minutes):
    • Complete a satisfaction survey at the end of the experiment.

POTENTIAL RISKS AND DISCOMFORTS

We do not forsee any risks in participating in this study.

POTENTIAL BENEFITS

If proven to be useful as a result of this study, Data Tamer will be used to help improve the quality of data curation techniques used by NIBR to manage Pharon assay data.

PAYMENT FOR PARTICIPATION

No payment or compensation will be offered for completing this study.

CONFIDENTIALITY

Any information that is obtained in connection with this study and that can be identified with you will remain confidential and will be disclosed only with your permission or as required by law.

Your interactions with Data Tamer (answers to data integration tasks) will be recorded through the website, and your responses to the questionnaires will be saved.

Data obtained from participants will be stored on a server located on Novartis’s campus. The server will be hosted within Novartis’s secure intranet. As such, access to the user interface will be restricted to authorized Novartis employees with appropriate credentials. The data will not be destroyed after analysis, and will remain on the server with the same security measures described above.
